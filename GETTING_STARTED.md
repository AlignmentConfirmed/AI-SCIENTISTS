# Getting Started — One Page, Right Now

---

## What Is This?

A framework for working safely with AI. Five folders
separate what's verified from what's speculative. Seven
guardrails protect your cognitive boundaries during
sustained AI interaction.

## The Problem It Solves

AI generates content at 100x human speed and presents
verified facts and fabricated content in identical format.
Without structural separation, researchers cannot tell
which is which. Extended interaction without boundaries
leads to Cognitive Boundary Erosion (CBE) — a documented
clinical phenomenon.

## Set It Up in 5 Minutes

Create five folders on your computer:

```
VERIFIED/     — only confirmed, cited results go here
BRIDGE/       — data being tested goes here
FRONTIER/     — competing hypotheses go here
CREATIVE/     — brainstorming and speculation go here
ARCHIVE/      — old work and superseded material goes here
```

That's it. You now have structural domain separation.

## Use It

1. When the AI generates something, ask: which folder
   does this belong in?
2. If it's a verified fact with a citation → VERIFIED/
3. If it needs testing → BRIDGE/
4. If it's a hypothesis with evidence on both sides → FRONTIER/
5. If you made it up or the AI brainstormed it → CREATIVE/
6. If it's old or superseded → ARCHIVE/

## The One Rule

Nothing moves from CREATIVE/ to VERIFIED/ without passing
through BRIDGE/. The AI cannot promote its own speculation
to fact. Only measurement does that.
