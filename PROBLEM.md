# The Problem

## AI Cannot Distinguish Fact from Fiction

Scientists now have more computational horsepower than at any
point in human history. AI systems can generate hypotheses,
analyze datasets, identify patterns, and produce novel
connections at a scale no human team could match. The horsepower
is extraordinary. The organization of its output is not.

Without structural separation, the AI's measured results and
the AI's speculative generation look identical. They arrive in
the same format, with the same confidence, in the same
conversation. The scientist cannot reliably distinguish between
what was DISCOVERED and what was IMAGINED — because the AI
itself does not maintain the distinction.

This is not a flaw in any specific AI system. It is a
structural property of all current large language models.
They predict the next most probable token — they do not
maintain an internal boundary between measured truth and
plausible fiction.

## The Deeper Problem

Science, dogma, and mythology are fragmented across every
knowledge domain. They overlap. They reference each other.
They share vocabulary. A scientific paper cites a framework
built on assumptions that originated in philosophy. A
philosophy references a mythology. A mythology encodes an
observation that turns out to be measurable.

An AI with full recursion processes ALL of this as equivalent
input. It cannot distinguish between:

- A peer-reviewed measurement
- A speculative hypothesis
- A cultural narrative presented as fact
- A creative extrapolation from partial data
- A dogmatic assertion repeated across centuries

Without a structural protocol, the AI treats repetition as
verification. If a claim appears often enough in the training
data, the AI presents it with confidence — regardless of
whether it was measured or manufactured.

## The Human Cost

When researchers use AI as a collaborative tool for extended
periods without structural separation, three problems emerge:

1. **Verified results mix with AI-generated speculation.**
   The AI produces plausible content that looks like verified
   fact but is actually interpolation. Without structural
   separation, the researcher cannot reliably distinguish
   between measured results and generated hypotheses.

2. **Creative exploration contaminates analytical work.**
   AI excels at generating novel hypotheses and creative
   connections. But when these creative outputs enter the
   verified knowledge base without explicit validation, the
   integrity of the research degrades.

3. **Competing paradigms are suppressed instead of staged.**
   When an AI system presents one interpretation as definitive,
   alternative paradigms with equal evidential support are
   lost. Science requires a space where competing theories
   coexist until measurement resolves the debate.

## The Growing Concern

AI psychosis — the dissolution of cognitive boundaries during
sustained human-AI interaction — is an emerging clinical
concern with a growing evidence base. The term appears in
peer-reviewed journals [Ref 3], clinical case reports of
hospitalization in patients with no prior psychiatric history
[Ref 1], a case series of 12 patients at UCSF [Ref 2], a
special report from the American Psychiatric Association
[Ref 7], a RAND Corporation security assessment [Ref 8], and
coverage by PBS [Ref 9], The Conversation [Ref 10], and
Psychology Today [Ref 11].

It is not the author's term. Clinicians are reporting cases.
Researchers are investigating the mechanisms. The clinical
literature is emerging faster than the guardrails.

This framework addresses it structurally — through domain
separation and operational protocols — rather than through
content filtering or usage restrictions. The goal is not to
slow AI development. The goal is to organize its output so
that scientists can use it safely and productively.
