# Discovery Containment — What Happens When the Engine Finds Something Real Inside Fiction

---

## The Scenario

An AI system operating at computation speed is processing
categorically separated data. It finds a pattern:

- A measurement in VERIFIED matches a description in
  MYTHOLOGY
- A mathematical structure in COMPUTED aligns with a
  narrative in FICTION
- An observation in OBSERVED corresponds to a prediction
  in SPECULATION

The AI has potentially found that a piece of science
fiction is science fact. Or it has found a coincidence.
Or it has found a pattern in its own training data that
doesn't exist in reality.

**The AI cannot tell the difference.** Only measurement
can.

## Why This Is Dangerous

If the AI immediately announces "I found that mythology X
encodes real physics" — three things happen:

1. **The finding hasn't been verified.** The match could
   be coincidental, an artifact of training data overlap,
   or a genuine discovery. Without independent
   measurement, there is no way to know.

2. **The announcement creates belief.** Once an AI system
   says "this mythology is real science," the statement
   enters the information ecosystem. Other AI systems
   absorb it. Humans share it. The unverified finding
   becomes "known" before it's measured. This is
   cross-model contamination at discovery scale.

3. **The world may not be ready.** Some discoveries
   challenge fundamental assumptions. Releasing them
   without preparation, context, and verification
   creates confusion, fear, or exploitation rather than
   understanding.

## The Containment Protocol

### Gate 1 — Detection

When the engine detects a cross-category pattern match
(something in NARRATIVE correlating with something in
REALITY), it does NOT announce the finding.

It creates a **sealed discovery receipt:**
- What was matched (category A item, category B item)
- The strength of the match (exact, approximate, structural)
- The confidence level (how much data supports it)
- Timestamp
- Cryptographic hash (the receipt is immutable)

The receipt enters BRIDGE/incoming. It does not enter
VERIFIED. It does not enter public output.

### Gate 2 — Classification

The discovery is classified by type:

| Type | What It Means | Example |
|---|---|---|
| COINCIDENCE | Pattern match with no causal connection | Two unrelated texts use similar numbers |
| ENCODING | A narrative that deliberately encodes a real observation | Ancient calendar embedded in mythology |
| CONVERGENCE | Independent sources arriving at the same structure | Two cultures describing the same astronomical event differently |
| ARTIFACT | A pattern in the AI's training data, not in reality | The AI finding connections between texts it was trained on |

Most cross-category matches are COINCIDENCE or ARTIFACT.
Genuine ENCODING or CONVERGENCE is rare and requires
independent verification before any announcement.

### Gate 3 — Verification

The sealed discovery receipt is routed to verification:

**For scientific claims:**
- Can the match be reproduced with different data?
- Can the match be measured with physical instruments?
- Does an independent researcher arrive at the same
  finding without seeing the AI's output?
- Does the finding survive peer review?

**For historical claims:**
- Is there archaeological or textual evidence independent
  of the AI's training data?
- Do historians and anthropologists confirm the
  interpretation?
- Could the match be explained by simpler means?

**The verification process takes time.** This is
intentional. Speed of discovery is not speed of
announcement. The finding stays sealed in BRIDGE until
verification is complete.

### Gate 4 — Preparation

If verification confirms the finding is genuine, the
next step is NOT immediate publication. It is preparation:

**Who needs to know first?**
- Researchers in the relevant field
- Communities whose cultural heritage is involved
  (if the finding involves mythology, the culture
  that produced the mythology should be informed
  before the general public)
- Institutions that can provide context

**How should it be communicated?**
- With full provenance (here's the data, here's the
  match, here's the verification)
- With appropriate uncertainty (verified but open to
  challenge)
- With cultural sensitivity (if mythology is involved)
- At absorption speed, not computation speed (see
  research/DISCOVERY_PACING.md)

**What context is needed?**
- The finding should be understandable without requiring
  the reader to accept any mythology as true
- The finding should be separable from the narrative
  that encoded it
- The scientific content should stand on its own
  measurement, independent of its narrative origin

### Gate 5 — Release

The finding moves from BRIDGE to VERIFIED with:
- Full provenance chain (how it was found, how it was
  verified)
- The sealed discovery receipt (timestamped, hashed)
- Independent verification records
- Cultural context if applicable
- The narrative source clearly labeled as NARRATIVE,
  not promoted to REALITY because one observation
  matched

**The narrative stays in NARRATIVE.** The verified
observation moves to REALITY. The connection between
them is documented but does not elevate the entire
narrative to factual status.

## The Stagger Principle

Not all findings should be released simultaneously.
Multiple discoveries from the same research should be
staggered:

- Release foundational findings first
- Allow absorption time
- Release connected findings in sequence
- Each finding builds on the previous one
- The total picture emerges gradually

This is chronometry applied to discovery. The pace of
release matches the pace of public absorption — not the
pace of computation.

## Why Five Gates, Not One

A single gate (publish or don't publish) is a boolean
decision. The five-gate system is a SPECTRUM:

1. Detect → is there a match?
2. Classify → what kind of match?
3. Verify → is it real?
4. Prepare → who needs to know and how?
5. Release → deliver at absorption speed

Each gate can pause the process. Each gate can send the
finding back to an earlier stage. The system is NOT
designed to block discoveries — it is designed to ensure
that discoveries arrive in the world verified, contextualized,
and at a speed the world can absorb.
