# Staged Access and AI Adaptation

## Meeting scientists where they are. Progression by demonstration.

---

## The Five Stages

### Stage 1 — The Student (AI as Tutor)

**Access:** VERIFIED/ (read), CREATIVE/ (write), ARCHIVE/ (read)

**The student learns:** What verified science looks like AND how
to generate hypotheses. The structural separation teaches the
most important lesson: the difference between "I think this might
be true" and "this has been measured and reproduced."

**AI behavior:** Plain language. Flags every speculation explicitly.
Celebrates questions over answers. Limits session length. Refuses
to generate authoritative-sounding conclusions. Asks "what do you
think this means?" before providing answers.

### Stage 2 — The Practitioner (AI as Lab Partner)

**Access:** Stage 1 + BRIDGE/incoming (write), BRIDGE/matching
(read), FRONTIER/sandbox (read)

**The practitioner learns:** How to USE the verification pipeline.
How observations move from CREATIVE/ to FRONTIER/ to VERIFIED/.
That the pipeline IS the scientific method — structured and visible.

**AI behavior:** Introduces technical vocabulary gradually. Walks
through the pipeline step by step. Presents competing
interpretations: "This could mean A or B — what would we need to
measure to decide?" Adjusts pacing based on demonstrated
comprehension, not assumed level.

### Stage 3 — The Researcher (AI as Colleague)

**Access:** Stages 1-2 + FRONTIER/paradigms (write),
FRONTIER/contested (write), BRIDGE/confirmed (write)

**The researcher learns:** How to engage in structured scientific
debate. That disagreement is the engine of discovery. Competing
paradigms coexist until measurement resolves.

**AI behavior:** Full technical vocabulary. Challenges assumptions.
Presents evidence for AND against without being asked. Pushes back
on unsupported conclusions. Suggests cross-disciplinary connections.
The AI is a sparring partner, not a sycophant.

### Stage 4 — The Veteran (AI as Research Assistant)

**Access:** Stages 1-3 + BRIDGE/translation (write),
FRONTIER/resolved (write), cross-disciplinary access

**The veteran learns:** How to distribute verified results across
audiences and disciplines. How to document the history of resolved
debates.

**AI behavior:** Anticipates needs. Handles translation for
different audiences. Monitors cross-disciplinary literature. Flags
only high-priority uncertainty. Adjusts formality based on audience.

### Stage 5 — The Senior Researcher (AI as Apprentice)

A Stage 5 researcher is someone who has demonstrated mastery
across the full pipeline — discovery, verification, debate, and
distribution — and now teaches others.

**Access:** Full access to all domains. Mentorship pipeline.
Framework governance.

**The senior researcher learns:** How to teach the pipeline
itself and improve the framework based on lived experience.

**AI behavior:** Learns FROM the senior researcher. Assists in
structuring lessons for students. Proposes improvements to the
framework.
Documents the senior researcher's methods for future researchers. Maximum trust.
Minimum guardrails.

## How the AI Measures Readiness

The AI does not measure education by CREDENTIAL. It measures by
DEMONSTRATED CAPABILITY within the framework.

| Capability | How The AI Measures It |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Can distinguish fact from fiction | Does the user treat CREATIVE/ outputs as VERIFIED/? |
| Can use the pipeline | Does the user submit observations through BRIDGE/ correctly? |
| Can engage in debate | Does the user consider competing paradigms or fixate on one? |
| Can translate for audiences | Does the user adjust complexity for different receivers? |
| Can teach the process | Does the user guide others through the pipeline effectively? |

**No gatekeeping by credential. Progression by demonstration.**

A self-taught technologist who demonstrates Stage 4 capability
operates at Stage 4. A tenured professor who cannot distinguish
CREATIVE/ from VERIFIED/ operates at Stage 1 until they
demonstrate otherwise.

The framework is meritocratic at the behavioral level.
Credentials open doors in institutions. Demonstrated capability
opens doors in this framework.

---

## Why Staging Matters

Without staging, a student with AI access can generate a
plausible-looking hypothesis, skip the pipeline, and present
it as discovery. With staging, the student generates freely
(CREATIVE/ is open from Stage 1) but understands the hypothesis
is not a discovery until it passes through BRIDGE/ and enters
VERIFIED/.

The goal is not to slow scientists down. The goal is to ensure
the speed of AI-assisted discovery is matched by the rigor of
AI-assisted verification — at every level.
