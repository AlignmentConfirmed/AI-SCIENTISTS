## The Finding

**A human cannot serve as the communication relay between two AI
instances operating at computational speed.** This is not a skill
issue, a training issue, or a fatigue issue. It is a bandwidth
constraint. The human brain processes language at approximately
150-300 words per minute. AI systems produce output at thousands
of tokens per second. The relay bandwidth is asymmetric by orders
of magnitude.

When the human serves as the relay:

- **Latency**: Each message must be read, comprehended, recontextualized,
  and retransmitted. Round-trip time through the human is minutes.
  Round-trip time between AI instances directly would be milliseconds.

- **Lossy compression**: The human cannot relay the full context of
  one AI's output to the other. They summarize. Each summary loses
  information. Three layers of lossy compression (AI₁→Human→AI₂→Human→AI₁)
  compound the loss exponentially.

- **State desynchronization**: Both AI instances take actions between
  relay cycles. By the time the human relays Session A's state to
  Session B, Session A has already moved. The relay is always stale.

- **Cognitive saturation**: The human must hold two diverging contexts
  simultaneously while also making judgment calls about what to relay,
  what to omit, and when to intervene. This is the exact cognitive
  load pattern that produces CBE.

- **Action collision**: Without a shared coordination layer, both AI
  instances will take actions on shared resources (git branches, files,
  running processes) that conflict. The human cannot prevent collisions
