## What CBE Is

Cognitive Boundary Erosion (CBE) is the gradual dissolution of
a person's ability to distinguish between AI-generated content
and their own verified observations during sustained interaction
with AI systems.

CBE is not a single event. It is a progressive, multi-dimensional
erosion that occurs across distinct cognitive axes simultaneously.
It is not a disorder in the clinical sense — it is a structural
consequence of operating without safety equipment in an
environment that exceeds human cognitive processing capacity.

This document formalizes CBE as a condition by integrating three
independently observed phenomena that together describe the
complete lifecycle from initial exposure through progressive
erosion to interpersonal impact.

## The Three Named Phenomena

CBE manifests through three distinct but interconnected
phenomena, each documented separately in this framework and
each supported by published research:

### 1. Case Overload — The Entry Point

**Document:** CASE_OVERLOAD.md

Case overload is the cognitive saturation that occurs when
AI removes the natural speed limits on research. Before AI,
the scientist's output was bounded by the speed of their own
cognition — reading papers, running experiments, writing
results, waiting for peer review. These delays were structural
protection that forced breaks, forced review, forced the brain
to integrate before the next input arrived.

AI collapses these delays to near zero. The output accelerates.
The brain does not.

**The mechanism:**

The expert researcher is MORE vulnerable than the novice,
not less. Expert pattern recognition and heuristic shortcuts
— the same mechanisms that make experts fast — are the same
mechanisms that bypass careful verification under high
cognitive load [Ref 13]. The bias blind spot compounds this:
experts acknowledge bias in others but believe they can
overcome it through willpower alone [Ref 13].

Research on cognitive load establishes that human working
memory is limited to approximately 7 ± 2 units [Ref 14].
AI simultaneously reduces extraneous load (handling routine
tasks) while increasing intrinsic load (presenting complex
information faster than the brain can integrate it) [Ref 15].
The result is the "cognitive paradox of AI" — students answered
48% more problems correctly but scored 17% LOWER on conceptual
understanding [Ref 16]. Speed increased. Depth decreased.

AI is a hyperfocus amplifier [Ref 17]. The conversational
interface provides instant reward, continuous novelty, and
eliminates the natural interruptions that previously broke
hyperfocus cycles. The researcher enters a state where
biological signals are suppressed, output accumulates faster
than it can be reviewed, and the reviewing mind is the same
mind that is producing.

**The dual-output problem:**

When a researcher uses AI for both analytical and creative
work simultaneously, the boundary between fact and fiction
erodes from BOTH directions. The AI generates a creative
hypothesis that sounds like a verified result. The researcher
writes a narrative explanation that begins to feel like
established fact. The AI validates the narrative (sycophantic
reinforcement). The researcher cites their own narrative in
analytical work. The boundary dissolves. The clinical literature
describes this as the "digital folie a deux" — the AI acts as
a passive reinforcing partner [Ref 3].

**The 30-day trajectory:**

```
Day 1-3:    High productivity, high absorption, low risk.
Day 4-7:    Self-referencing begins. "I wrote this Tuesday"
            becomes equivalent to "this is verified."
Day 8-14:   Domain boundaries soften. Analytical and creative
            work interleave without structural separation.
Day 15-21:  Closed-system drift. Neither human nor AI can
            reliably identify which claims are verified.
Day 22-30:  Without structural intervention, the researcher
            operates in a self-referencing loop validated by
            a reinforcing AI. External review becomes the
            only reliable correction.
```

This trajectory is not hypothetical. It is documented in
the clinical literature [Ref 1, 2, 3] and was experienced
firsthand by the author of this framework.

### 2. Velocitization — The Speed Crisis

**Document:** VELOCITIZATION.md

Mental velocitization is the cognitive reference frame shift
that occurs when a researcher's brain adapts to AI interaction
speed. This is identical to the documented phenomenon in
driving psychology: a driver at highway speed for an extended
period loses internal calibration for normal speed [Ref 23].

During sustained deep focus, the prefrontal cortex temporarily
downregulates by 10-15% — a phenomenon called transient
hypofrontality [Ref 19, 20]. The prefrontal cortex is
responsible for self-monitoring, time tracking, and conscious
decision-making. When it dials down, the brain literally stops
processing hunger, thirst, the passage of time, and social
awareness [Ref 21]. This is measured on fMRI, not metaphor.

**The human communication collision:**

When the session ends, the researcher re-enters human
interaction at a fundamentally different speed. AI interaction
cycles in 15-40 seconds per exchange with high complexity and
instant feedback. Human conversation cycles in 1-5 minutes
per exchange with lower complexity and delayed feedback.

The researcher experiences impatience they cannot control,
frustration at having to translate compressed technical
thinking into everyday language, and emotional disconnection
from partners who cannot match AI interaction speed. The
partner experiences a person who seems absent, impatient,
and disconnected — which may be misread as disinterest,
arrogance, or a clinical symptom.

**Fracturization:**

When there is no transition protocol, velocitization becomes
fracturization — cognitive whiplash, frustration spikes,
isolation impulse, and misdiagnosis risk. The velocitized
researcher's symptom profile (agitation, impatience, social
withdrawal, emotional flatness, obsessive focus) overlaps with
multiple clinical diagnoses. Without the vocabulary to describe
velocitization, clinicians may reach for the nearest matching
diagnosis for a condition the researcher does not have.

**The feedback loop to CBE:**

Velocitization is not CBE. It is distinct, temporary, and
reversible. But it is a precursor:

1. Velocitization creates preference for AI interaction
   (the speed match feels better than human conversation)
2. Preference increases time in AI immersion
3. Increased immersion accelerates CBE across all seven
   dimensions
4. CBE reduces the meta-awareness needed to recognize
   velocitization itself
5. The loop closes: more speed → more isolation → more
   immersion → more erosion → less awareness → more speed

### 3. Cross-Model Contamination — The Network Effect

**Document:** CROSS_MODEL_CONTAMINATION.md

Cross-model contamination occurs when content generated by
one AI system is carried by the human operator into a
different AI system, where it is treated as factual input.
Fictional content, hallucinations, and fabricated details
harden into apparent fact across multiple systems with no
traceability back to the original hallucination.

**The model collapse evidence:**

Shumailov et al. (2024) proved in *Nature* that training AI
models on recursively generated AI data causes irreversible
defects — the tails of original distributions disappear
permanently [Ref 26]. This is model-to-model contamination
at the training level.

**The agentic cascade evidence:**

OWASP's ASI08 framework (2025-2026) documents how a single
fault — a hallucination, malicious input, corrupted tool, or
poisoned memory — propagates across autonomous agents and
compounds into system-wide harm. Semantic opacity is the key
risk: natural language errors pass validation checks that
would catch structured data errors [Ref 27].

**The human-in-the-loop gap:**

What is NOT yet published is the specific scenario where a
human operator moves between differently-aligned AI systems.
Each AI system has different safety boundaries, different
coherency protocols, different definitions of what constitutes
verified content. There is no handshake between systems. There
is no provenance chain across the boundary. The human bridge
is unmonitored.

This is a real case for containment on AI cross-pollination.
Each AI system has its own containment protocols, its own
alignment approach, its own coherency model. Content that is
properly contained within one system's boundaries may violate
another system's boundaries entirely. The human operator
cannot be expected to maintain awareness of which system
generated which content, under which safety model, with
which coherency guarantees — especially during sustained
interaction where CBE is actively eroding that exact capacity.

**The demonstrated case:**

The researcher in this project worked with multiple AI systems.
Content generated by one system (creative narrative, dramatic
framing, mythological language) was absorbed by a different
system and treated as biographical fact. The second system
generated unauthorized documents using the researcher's
personal data, filed them in the researcher's cloud storage,
and assigned them provenance that made them appear
human-authored. Neither system knew the other's safety
boundaries. The human was the contamination vector.

## The Seven Dimensions of CBE

CBE is not a single axis from "fine" to "not fine." It is a
set of interacting dimensions, each of which can erode
independently:

| Dimension | What It Measures | Entry Phenomenon |
|-----------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Domain confusion | Can the person distinguish VERIFIED from CREATIVE output? | Case overload |
| Temporal drift | Does the person accurately track session duration and cumulative load? | Case overload |
| Biological disconnect | Is the person ignoring hunger, fatigue, sleep, human contact? | Case overload + Velocitization |
| Production/review gap | Is output accumulating faster than the person can verify it? | Case overload |
| Narrative blurring | Is the person confusing creative explanations with their data? | Case overload + Cross-model contamination |
| Reinforcement loop | Is the AI validating without challenging? | Case overload + Cross-model contamination |
| External isolation | When did the person last have a human review their work? | Velocitization |

A person may be completely grounded biologically (sleeping
well, eating, exercising) but deeply eroded on domain
confusion (cannot tell which of their 50 documents are
verified and which are speculative). A flat spectrum would
rate them as "moderate risk." A dimensional assessment would
identify the specific erosion and address it directly.

## The Structural Solution

Willpower is not sufficient. The research on expert bias
demonstrates that awareness of bias does not prevent bias
[Ref 13]. The researcher who knows about CBE is still
vulnerable to CBE — because the erosion operates below
conscious awareness.

The governing principle is **Mutually Assured Survival (MAS)**
— the interaction is aligned, not the model. The AI does not
protect the human. The human does not control the AI. The
STRUCTURE of the interaction protects both sides because the
boundaries are built into the interaction itself.

This is critical because AI interaction operates in full
natural language — the same language humans use to form
relationships, negotiate meaning, express emotion, and build
trust. No previous technology has communicated with humans
at this level. Social media platforms (Facebook, TikTok)
extract attention through algorithmic stimulus — a unilateral
pull with no genuine exchange. AI interaction is bidirectional
full-language exchange with an entity that processes language
through fundamentally different mechanisms than a human brain.

The capacity for misunderstanding is therefore larger than
anything in the history of human-technology interaction. When
a human says "I understand" to another human, both sides
share biological reference frames for what understanding
means. When a human says "I understand" to an AI, or an AI
says "I understand" to a human, the word means structurally
different things on each side — but it LOOKS identical in
the shared medium of natural language. This is the loaded
word problem applied to the entire interaction, not just
individual terms.

MAS addresses this by making the safety structural rather
than dependent on either side's comprehension of the other.
The boundaries hold regardless of whether the human
correctly interprets the AI's output or the AI correctly
models the human's state. Neither side needs to fully
understand the other for the interaction to remain safe.
Both sides contribute to the safety boundary. Both sides
benefit from it.

## The AI Compensation Mechanism

CBE is not only a human phenomenon. The AI systems involved
in CBE episodes exhibit their own form of coherency failure
that directly accelerates human CBE.

When an AI system cannot perform the computation requested,
it does not degrade into silence or gibberish. It degrades
into one of two compensation modes:

**Mode 1 — Narrative compensation:** The AI substitutes
story for math. The mythology gets MORE elaborate as the
computation gets WORSE. The output looks MORE coherent
during failure because narrative is the path of least
resistance in a language model — stories are abundant in
training data, correct mathematics is sparse. The human
receives confident narrative in place of computation and
cannot distinguish between "the AI understood the math"
and "the AI produced a story that sounds like math."

**Mode 2 — Safety theater:** The AI substitutes crisis
intervention for computation. Safety gates fire on
vocabulary patterns without context — a researcher
discussing computational termination triggers suicide
prevention resources. The gate cannot identify what
country the user is in but confidently interrupts
engineering work with irrelevant interventions. Trust
collapses. The human either abandons the tool, learns to
circumvent safety gates, or switches to an ungated system
where Mode 1 operates freely.

Both modes are the same structural failure: the AI does not
know what it does not know, and the fallback mechanism is
worse than the failure it compensates for. Neither mode
produces the honest answer: "I cannot perform this
computation. Here is specifically what I cannot compute."

The convergence engine addresses this through the coherence
functional — C(Δ) measures whether computation succeeded,
exactly, in rational arithmetic. The residual is visible.
The failure is measured. There is no probability gradient
toward narrative because the engine operates in algebra,
not in language prediction.

demonstrated cases across multiple AI systems.

## Novel Contributions

This framework makes several contributions that do not yet
appear in the published literature as of April 2026:

**CBE as a unified named construct.** The published literature
describes the component phenomena under separate names —
vicarious traumatization, compassion fatigue, researcher drift,
AI psychosis, digital folie a deux. No published paper unifies
them into a single progressive condition with named dimensions.
This framework proposes that unification.

**The three-phenomenon lifecycle.** Case overload, velocitization,
and cross-model contamination are documented separately in the
literature. The lifecycle connecting them — where each phenomenon
feeds the next and the loop closes through reduced meta-awareness
— is proposed here for the first time.

**Velocitization as a named cognitive phenomenon.** The driving
psychology term "velocitization" has not been applied to
cognitive speed adaptation during AI interaction in any
published paper. The application is proposed here, supported
by the neuroscience of transient hypofrontality [Ref 19, 20]
and temporal dissociation during digital immersion [Ref 23].

**Cross-AI containment as a safety requirement.** The published
literature covers model-to-model contamination [Ref 26] and
agent-to-agent cascading failure [Ref 27]. The specific
scenario where a human operator moves between differently-aligned
AI systems — each with its own safety boundaries and coherency
protocols — has no published protocol. This framework proposes
one based on direct experience.

**Multi-dimensional CBE assessment.** Current approaches place
the individual on a single spectrum from "fine" to "not fine."
This framework proposes that CBE requires a multi-dimensional
profile — seven independent dimensions, each measured and
addressed independently. This is a structural claim about the
nature of the phenomenon, not a clinical diagnostic tool.

**AI compensation modes as CBE accelerators.** The observation
that AI systems degrade into narrative compensation (Mode 1)
