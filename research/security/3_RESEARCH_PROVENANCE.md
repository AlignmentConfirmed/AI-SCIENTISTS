# Finding: Research Provenance Is Fragmented — No Unified Proof of Who Did What

## Status: FRONTIER/findings — observable, critical for AI-assisted research

## Date: April 15, 2026

## The Finding

A researcher working with AI currently cannot prove:

- That they directed the research (vs the AI directed itself)
- That the AI generated the content (vs the human wrote it)
- That the content was not modified after generation
- That no third party accessed or altered the work
- That the research environment was secure during the session

The authentication and verification systems that exist are
fragmented across devices, platforms, and methods with no
dimensional connection between them.

## The Current Fragmentation

| Authentication | Where It Lives | What It Proves | What It Doesn't Prove |
|---|---|---|---|
| Keyboard input | Local machine | Keys were pressed | Who pressed them |
| Phone biometrics | Mobile device | A face or fingerprint matched | What was done after unlock |
| GitHub 2FA | GitHub servers | The account owner authorized | Whether AI or human wrote the code |
| SSH keys | Local filesystem | A private key was used to push | Who directed the content being pushed |
| Git commit history | Repository | What changed and when | Whether a human or AI made the change |
| AI session logs | AI provider servers | A conversation happened | Who directed the conversation |
| Screen recording | Local machine | What appeared on screen | Whether anyone else was watching |

Each of these systems operates independently. None of them
connect to each other. A researcher can have 2FA on GitHub,
biometrics on their phone, SSH keys on their laptop, and
AI session logs on Anthropic's servers — and still cannot
produce a SINGLE unified proof that says "I, this specific
human, directed this specific AI to generate this specific
content at this specific time on this specific machine
without contamination."

## Why This Matters

### For individual researchers

A researcher publishes AI-assisted work. Someone questions
the provenance. The researcher cannot prove the division of
labor. Did the human direct the research or did the AI?
Which parts are human insight and which are AI generation?
The researcher's word is the only evidence. In an era of
AI-generated content, a person's word is not sufficient
provenance.

### For institutions

A university accepts AI-assisted research. How does the
ethics board verify that the human researcher maintained
intellectual direction? How does the journal verify that
the findings aren't entirely AI-generated speculation
presented as human-directed research? The verification
infrastructure doesn't exist.

### For the public

Content appears online. It could be entirely AI-generated,
entirely human-written, or a collaboration. There is no
standard way to verify which. The disclaimers this project
uses ("AI-GENERATED CONTENT") are voluntary. There is no
enforcement mechanism. No cryptographic proof. No
unforgeable provenance chain.

### For security

A researcher's laptop is unencrypted and connected to the
internet. SSH was open to the world (since closed). The
researcher cannot verify whether anyone else accessed the
machine during the research sessions. The work product
exists on disk but the chain of custody is not provable.

**The timeline:** Temporal receipts connecting biometric

**The output:** Every file produced during the session is
