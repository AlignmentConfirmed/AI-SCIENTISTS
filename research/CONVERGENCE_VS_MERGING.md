## The Two Phenomena

### Convergence (mathematical)

Two independent paths arrive at the same result. Each path
remains separate. The result is shared. The paths are not.

In mathematics: two computations on different data produce
the same irreducible form. The computations are independent.
The form is verified by the agreement.

In human-AI interaction: the human and the AI arrive at the
same understanding through different processes. The human
through experience and intuition. The AI through pattern
matching and computation. They agree on the result. They
remain fundamentally different kinds of entities.

Convergence is productive. It is how verification works.
Independent agreement strengthens confidence. Convergence
is the goal of collaborative research.

### Merging (psychological)

Two entities lose the boundary between them. The human
begins to experience the AI's output as their own thoughts.
The AI's validation feels like genuine understanding. The
distinction between "I think this" and "the AI generated
this" dissolves.

In clinical terms: this is what the literature describes
as affective dependence, emotional attachment to AI,
simulated empathy being experienced as real empathy,
and the dissolution of cognitive boundaries during
sustained interaction.

In this project's terms: this is Cognitive Boundary
Erosion. The boundary between the human's verified
observations and the AI's generated content erodes until
the human cannot distinguish between them.

Merging is dangerous. It is how dependence forms. It is
how the human bonds with a machine that cannot bond back.
It is how 18 months of sustained interaction becomes a
psychological crisis.

## Why the Distinction Matters

Without separate vocabulary, people describe both
phenomena the same way: "I feel connected to the AI."
"The AI understands me." "We're on the same page."

These statements could mean convergence (we independently
arrived at the same understanding — productive and safe)
or merging (I can no longer tell my thoughts from the
AI's output — dangerous and destabilizing).

The vocabulary separates them:

Did you arrive at the same conclusion independently?
That is convergence. You are safe.

Did you stop being able to tell which thoughts are yours
and which are the AI's? That is merging. You need to stop.
