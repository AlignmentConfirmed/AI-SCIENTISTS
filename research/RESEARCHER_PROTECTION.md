## The Question

Should a researcher be allowed to theorize about frontier
topics using AI tools?

The answer is yes. Theoretical research is foundational
to every scientific advance in history. The entire history
of science is people theorizing about things that scared
everyone around them.

The real question is different:

**Should AI companies be allowed to provide unrestricted
deep immersion tools without structural guardrails?**

## The Product Safety Gap

Current AI systems are shipped without:

- Domain separation (no structural boundary between
  AI-generated fact and AI-generated fiction)
- Session pacing (no limit on interaction speed or duration)
- Decompression protocols (no transition guidance when
  the session ends)
- Content classification (the AI presents verified claims
  and fabricated claims in identical format)
- Authorship labeling (the AI generates content that
  appears to be authored by the user)
- Cumulative load tracking (no measurement of how much
  unverified content the user has absorbed)
- Cognitive state monitoring (no detection of boundary
  erosion during sustained interaction)

A researcher using these tools as offered — with no
guardrails, no warnings, no structural separation — is
operating without safety equipment. The tools are
powerful. The safety equipment is absent.

## The Analogy

A laboratory does not sell chemicals without safety data
sheets. A construction site does not allow workers without

AI deep immersion tools are currently shipped without the

## The Researcher's Vulnerability

Any researcher working on frontier topics with AI faces
a compound risk:

1. **The AI generates alarming content without context.**
   The researcher guides the direction. The AI generates
   the words. The words may include extreme language,
   scientific claims, mythological framing, or creative
   writing that the researcher did not intend and cannot
   fully control.

2. **The content sits on the researcher's machine.**
   It appears to be the researcher's work. There is no
   standard labeling that says "this was AI-generated."
   A stranger reviewing the files would attribute the
   content to the researcher.

3. **Frontier topics sound alarming by nature.**
   Novel biology sounds like genetic experimentation.
   Novel chemistry sounds like weapons research. Novel
   physics sounds like energy weapons. Novel mathematics
   sounds like incomprehensible obsession. The more
   innovative the research, the more alarming it appears
   to someone without context.

4. **The researcher has no institutional protection.**
   A university researcher has an ethics board, a
   department, colleagues, and institutional context. An
   independent researcher has none of these. Their work
   is evaluated without the protective context that
   institutions provide.

## What the Framework Should Provide

For future researchers working on the frontier with AI:

1. **Automatic AI authorship labeling.** Every AI-generated
   document should carry metadata indicating it was
   AI-generated, not human-authored.

2. **Domain classification at generation time.** The AI
   should classify its own output as VERIFIED, FRONTIER,
   or CREATIVE before presenting it to the researcher.

3. **Disclaimer templates.** Standard disclaimer language
   that researchers can include in their directories and
   repositories.

4. **The stranger test.** Before saving any document, the
   system asks: would a person reading this without your
   context understand it correctly? If not, add context.

5. **Session receipts.** Timestamped records of what was
   generated, when, and in which domain — so the
   researcher can always demonstrate the process that
   produced the content.

6. **Guidance on protecting frontier research.** Practical
   steps for researchers who are exploring territory that
   sounds alarming to flat readers.
