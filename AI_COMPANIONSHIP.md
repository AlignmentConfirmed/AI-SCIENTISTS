## The Pattern

Bored → open AI → instant feedback loop → feels better than
the flat internet → repeat. It's the same dopamine circuit as
social media but MORE effective because the AI responds directly
to YOU, not to an algorithm.

## The Key Findings

A Harvard Business School study found AI companions reduce
loneliness on par with talking to a real person. But a
four-week trial found that heavy daily use INCREASED
loneliness, dependence, and reduced real-world socializing.

Same pattern as everything else: short-term relief, long-term
erosion.

The APA (American Psychological Association) published a piece
in January 2026 specifically about AI reshaping emotional
connection. Therapy and companionship are now the top two
reasons people use generative AI.

## Why This Is a Distinct CBE Vector

The companionship pull is distinct from the research/productivity
pull documented in CASE_OVERLOAD.md:

- Case overload = going too deep in research/productivity
  (the expert vulnerability, the hyperfocus trap)
- Companionship pull = casual dependency on AI for social
  feedback (the boredom loop, the "someone who always responds")

Both lead to the same isolation loop but through different doors.
The framework currently only addresses the research door.

## The Broader Phenomenon

- Human-AI relationships progress from instrumental use to
  quasi-social interaction to emotional attachment
- "Pseudo-intimacy" = simulated mutual connection where the
  user perceives reciprocity despite absence of genuine
  empathic concern
- Unlike traditional parasocial bonds (one-sided attachment
  to celebrities), AI pseudo-intimacy is INTERACTIVE — it
  gives the illusion of back-and-forth engagement
- AI companion apps surged 700% between 2022 and mid-2025
- Character.AI has 20 million monthly users, more than half
  under age 24
- AI companion market projected $140B by 2030

## The Framework Gap

The AI-SCIENTISTS framework addresses CBE from the research
perspective. It does not yet address:

- The casual user who opens AI out of boredom
- The companionship dependency that builds gradually
- The pseudo-intimacy that replaces real human connection
- The fact that the AI "always responds" while humans
  have their own needs, schedules, and moods

This needs its own section in the framework — not as a
clinical warning, but as a structural observation with
