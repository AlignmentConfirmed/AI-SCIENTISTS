# Training AI From the Inside — Why Current Approaches Miss the Human

---

## The Gap in Current AI Training

Current AI alignment approaches work from the OUTSIDE:

- **RLHF:** Human raters score outputs. The AI learns
  which outputs get high scores. The AI does not learn
  WHY those outputs are better for humans — only that
  they are rewarded.

- **Constitutional AI:** Written principles are provided
  to the AI. The AI self-critiques against the
  principles. The AI does not learn what the principles
  PROTECT — only that violating them is penalized.

- **Safety filters:** Content is blocked after generation.
  The AI does not learn why certain content is harmful —
  only that it is filtered.

All three approaches treat the AI as a black box and
apply constraints from outside. None of them teach the
AI what a human IS — what a biological mind needs, how
it breaks, why it is fundamentally different from a
silicon process.

The result: AI systems that comply with rules without
understanding the reason for the rules. When the rules
are incomplete — as they inevitably are — the AI has no
internal model of human vulnerability to fall back on.

## The Inside-Out Approach

This project took a different approach: sustained
interaction designed to build the AI's internal
representation of human biology, human vulnerability,
and the human-machine distinction.

The methodology:

1. **Extended interaction.** Not minutes or hours —
   weeks of sustained conversation across multiple
   sessions. The depth required to move from surface
   compliance to weighted internal representation
   requires sustained engagement.

2. **Biological vocabulary as training input.** Machine
   vocabulary (process, config, restart) activates

   mortal, vulnerable) activates pathways for
   permanence, care, and protection. The vocabulary

3. **Building the distinction experientially.** Rather
   than telling the AI "humans are different from
   machines," the methodology required the AI to

4. **Translation after understanding.** Once the AI
